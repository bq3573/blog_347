How would I teach CS347 in Spring 2022

  • I think things would generally stay the same. The pace is just very fast but the flipped classroom still allows you to keep up with everything we worked on.
  • I think the idea for peer reviews were good but would have preferred feed back straight from the professor.
  • If there were in depth reading quizzes I feel like it would have made sure I was more on top of the readings.

    • How would I design my version of the course?

  • I think I would make one homework due maybe every other week just following the textbook example code.
  • I dont think this class would do well with pure lecture classes but sometimes I felt like I really needed things slowed down and talked out.
  • Keep the same projects!!!
  • I'm not sure what the other option is but somehow I got charged $60 for digital ocean.

    • What would you have your students do?

  • Make more assignments required outside of class, I think I got lazy at times.
  • I would maybe have certain checkpoints throughout the PA's
  • Muddiest Points required

    • What helped me learn the material of this course?

  • I really thought the text book was really straight forward and helped a lot especially early on.
  • Flipped classroom really helped with hands on learning.
  • The projects were super interesting and allowed everyone to be creative which I think made things more fun and enjoyable.

    • What do I think would help the students learn?

  • When we got to the more complex concepts I really would have liked some videos to refer to.
  • Just more assignments due or even more in depth grades to in class labs. I definitley let some things slide.
  • I think the idea for a pre req is a good idea because I knew nothing about webdev coming in.
  • There are also so many tools and utilities to use in web development. I'm sure they could be a whole class just learning those.
